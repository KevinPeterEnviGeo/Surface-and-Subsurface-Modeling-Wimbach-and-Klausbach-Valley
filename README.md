# Surface-and-Subsurface-Modeling-Wimbach-and-Klausbach-Valley

This project was conducted as part of a university field and research course focusing on the geomorphological investigation of the Wimbach and Klausbach valleys in Berchtesgaden National Park, Germany. The study explored both surface and subsurface characteristics of these alpine environments using a range of geophysical, geomatic, and geomorphological methods. The different approaches provided complementary perspectives on landscape structure and evolution, resulting in a diverse set of findings that are discussed in detail in the accompanying report.

## Overview

This repository contains selected materials from the project. Due to GitHub storage limitations, the majority of the GIS and terrestrial laser scanning (TLS) datasets cannot be shared through this repository. Additionally, the Electrical Resistivity Tomography (ERT) data were collected as part of a larger collaborative effort and are therefore not included.

The project focused on an in-depth geomorphological investigation of the Wimbach and Klausbach valleys. By combining surface mapping, geophysical surveys, remote sensing techniques, and spatial analysis, the study aimed to improve understanding of valley morphology, sediment dynamics, and subsurface structures within these alpine catchments.

While only a subset of the project data is available here, the repository provides access to the final report and selected supporting datasets that document the project's methodology and principal findings.

## Research Objectives

* Investigate the geomorphological characteristics of the Wimbach and Klausbach valleys.
* Explore relationships between surface processes and subsurface structures.
* Apply and evaluate multiple geomorphological and geophysical survey methods.
* Develop an integrated understanding of alpine landscape evolution.

## Methods

* Electrical Resistivity Tomography (ERT)
* Terrestrial Laser Scanning (TLS)
* GIS-based Analysis
* Geomorphic Mapping
* Structure-from-Motion (SfM)
